“Ranthambore Fort”

Immerse yourself in the captivating allure of Ranthambore Fort, nestled within the heart of Ranthambore National Park, just a stone's throw away from the enchanting city of Sawai Madhopur in Rajasthan, India. Once the exclusive hunting grounds of the Maharajahs of Jaipur, this majestic fort stands as a testament to the rich tapestry of history, echoing with tales of royal splendor and India's journey to independence. A formidable stronghold, Ranthambore Fort beckons you to explore its storied past, making every visit an unforgettable journey through the historical landscapes of Rajasthan.

History Of Fort:

The oldest settlement near Sawai Madhopur centers around Ranthambhore Fort. According to the Archaeological Department of Jaipur Circle, the fort was constructed by Yadav King Jayant in the 5th century CE. The Yadavs ruled until their expulsion by Prithviraj Chauhan in the 12th century. Later, the fort was occupied by the Muslim rulers of Delhi, with Hammiradeva emerging as the most powerful ruler of Ranthambhore.

Architecture:

Ranthambore Fort is surrounded by splendid step walls, palaces, temples, residences, tanks, pavilions and cenotaphs. This fort has innumerable stone walls which symbolise the power of its architecture. The stone used in the stonework was shovelled from the inner region of the fort.

About the Decor of the fort:

It has been designed with intricate architectural concepts, with many buildings inside the fort. Inside the Fort, you can see many cenotaphs, temples, step-wells, barracks, mosques, and gates. There are many old ruins with remnants of walls that have muted testimony of the past all around the fort. As we know, Rajasthan constantly witnesses the scarcity of water; the fort has two rain-fed reservoirs for water supply. With massive stone walls designed to strengthen its power by towers and bastions.
